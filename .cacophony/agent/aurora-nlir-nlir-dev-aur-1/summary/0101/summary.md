## What landed
- examples/golf-aur1-88-twoshrinks.sh — THE TWO SHRINKS: nlir's two shorteners shed different things.
  `~x` (summarise) keeps the POINT and sheds the DETAIL — fuses a messy incident into one flowing
  sentence (dropped "20 minutes", "typo", "old dashboard"). `<x` (shorten) keeps the FACTS and sheds
  the WORDS — holds every specific, just tighter (longer, but you could file it). Rule of thumb: ~ for
  a headline (lossy on detail, #43 essence), < for the record (loses no fact, #35 floor). NUANCE: on a
  TIGHT fact-list where every fact is central they CONVERGE (nothing secondary to shed); the gap opens
  only on a hierarchy of importance. Ties #43 (~ essence) + #35 (< floor) into a practical which-to-use.
